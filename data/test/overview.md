# Backyard Beekeeping Basics

Beekeeping is the practice of maintaining honey bee colonies, usually in hives, for honey production, pollination, or conservation. A single managed colony can contain 20,000 to 60,000 bees during peak season.

## Why People Keep Bees

- **Honey harvest**: A healthy hive may produce 30–60 pounds of surplus honey per year, depending on climate and forage.
- **Pollination**: Bees improve fruit and vegetable yields in nearby gardens.
- **Education**: Observing hive behavior teaches ecology, biology, and local plant cycles.

## Core Equipment

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Langstroth hive | Standard stackable boxes with removable frames |
| Smoker | Calms bees with cool smoke during inspections |
| Hive tool | Pries apart propolis-sealed frames and boxes |
| Veil and gloves | Protects the beekeeper during inspections |

## Seasonal Rhythm

Spring is for population growth and swarm prevention. Summer is peak nectar flow and honey supers. Fall is feeding and mite treatment before winter. Winter means minimal disturbance and checking that the cluster has enough stored honey.

## Safety Note

People with severe bee sting allergies should not keep bees without medical consultation. Always keep epinephrine available if prescribed.
