# Beekeeping FAQ

## How much does it cost to start?

A beginner setup—hive, tools, veil, two colonies—often runs $600–$1,200 USD in the first year including bees.

## How often should I inspect?

Every 7–10 days during spring buildup; every 2–3 weeks in summer; rarely in winter except brief checks on warm days.

## What is Varroa destructor?

Varroa is a parasitic mite that feeds on bee brood and transmits viruses. Most hobbyists monitor mite levels monthly and treat when counts exceed local thresholds (often 2–3 mites per 100 bees in alcohol wash).

## Can I keep bees in the city?

Many cities allow two to four hives with registration and setback rules. Rooftop beekeeping is common in urban areas with good forage from parks and gardens.

## How long do queens live?

Queens typically remain productive for 1–3 years. Requeening annually or biennially is common in commercial operations; hobbyists often requeen when brood patterns decline.

## Is beeswax useful?

Yes. Beeswax sells for candle making, cosmetics, and food wraps. A pound of rendered wax may sell for $8–$15 retail depending on purity and market.

## What plants help bees?

Late-season favorites include goldenrod, asters, and sedum. Early spring support comes from willow, maple, and dandelion—often considered weeds but valuable bee forage.
