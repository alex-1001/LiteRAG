# Setting Up Your First Hive

Most beginners start with two deep boxes (brood chambers) and later add honey supers above an inner cover.

## Site Selection

Place hives where they receive morning sun and afternoon shade in hot climates. Face the entrance southeast when possible. Provide a windbreak and a fresh water source within 100 feet—bees prefer shallow water with landing spots.

Avoid placing hives:

- Directly against property lines (check local ordinances)
- Under dripping gutters
- Where lawn mowers or foot traffic cross the flight path daily

## Installing a Package or Nucleus

A **package** is roughly 3 pounds of bees plus a caged queen. A **nuc** (nucleus colony) is a small established colony on five frames.

Typical package install steps:

1. Remove four center frames from the bottom brood box.
2. Suspend the queen cage between two frames with the candy end pointing up.
3. Shake the package bees into the hive around the queen cage.
4. Feed 1:1 sugar syrup until comb is drawn on at least eight frames.

Queens usually take 3–7 days to be released from a candy-plug cage.

## First Inspection Timeline

- **Day 3**: Confirm the queen was released from her cage.
- **Day 7–10**: Look for eggs and larva—proof the queen is laying.
- **Week 3+**: Assess brood pattern and add the second brood box when 7–8 frames are drawn.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

Opening the hive too often in the first month stresses the colony. Failing to control Varroa mites in the first year is the leading cause of winter loss for new beekeepers.
